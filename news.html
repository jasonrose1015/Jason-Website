Meltdown and Spectre

Meltdown and Spectre take advantage of critical vulnerabilities in processors. These weaknesses have been found in both AMD and Intel processors. While the major concern will be the newer CPUs, older CPUs are also vulnerable. AMD has said that their processors will be patched via a software update. However, Intel will need a software and firmware update. It is said that in some cases, the patches will reduce performance by thirty percent. For more,click here.

How to Properly Patch Meltdown and Spectre

While you've probably heard of the Meltdown and Spectre processor flaws, you may not know what you need to do to patch or fix your PC, Mac or mobile device. The process is different for each device. TechRadarhas a great article over how to correctly apply the update or patch including step by step guides.

Meltdown and Spectre Fake Patches Released

As many users scramble to update their PCs and Macs to protect against the Meltdown and Spectre threats, that threat has increased as malicious software is being released to appear as a patch, but actually installs malware. The fake patch is delivered as an EXE (Intel-AMD-SecurityPatch.exe) and when it runs, it infects the PC with the malware and causes chaos on the user's machine. For more, check outTechRadar'sarticle.

The Cooler Master H500P

It is a beautiful case. However, like many tech companies today, Cooler Master focused more on the aesthetics instead of the overall function or performance. After completing a build in it, I found that it is difficult to build in and does a very poor job of keeping the components cool. The entire PSU shroud must be removed to install a PSU. The massive 200mm fans are basically useless thanks to the front acrylic panel that restricts airflow. After my initial build, the lack of airflow led to overheating. I had to install addition fans in the front of the case to increase the amount of air being pulled in. Still, I must leave the front panel open to help improve the cooling. Other Tech Tubers also realized this and posted videos. At CES 2018, Cooler Master announced newer versions of this case to fix all of the issues. You can check it outhere.

WPA3 Bringing Stronger Encryption to Wireless Routers in 2018

Now is not the best time to buy a router. Although many router manufacturers are releasing faster speeds, the security is standard is becoming outdated. That is, until now. A replacement for WPA2, which has been around for far too long, is coming in 2018. Now that WPA2 has been broken, the update for the standard can't arrive soon enough. PCGamerhas a great article discussing WPA3.

New Malware Found on iMacs and Macbooks

New malware has been discovered on Apple's iMacs and Macbooks. Code named OSX/MaMi, the malware changes the DNS server settings on the victim's machine, redirecting their internet traffic through malicious servers designed to steal the user's data. The malware is capable of taking screenshots, downloading and uploading files, executing commands and configures the machine for a man-in-the-middle attack.
