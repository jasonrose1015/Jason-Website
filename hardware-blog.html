Even after Corsair announced a recall of their Dark Core RGB mouse, I purchased one. This is how it went.

by Jason Rose

Posted on May 15, 2018

When Corsair announced their new Dark Core RGB SE wireless mouse with built in Qi charging, I knew I had to get one. Combined with Corsair's Qi charging mouse pad (MM1000), you should always have a full battery to go with the freedom of no wires.

The Dark Core RGB SE can be connected to your PC in three different ways. You can connect it using the included USB cable, 2.4GHz USB adapter (1ms latency) or low-latency Bluetooth. Depending on your setup, the wireless devices you already use and the location of your wireless router, you may or may not experience connection issues.

Overall, I think the Corsair Dark Core RGB SE is great.

The only issues I have experienced so far is when the cursor randomly jumps to another part of the screen. It is not much of an issue when browsing the web or when I am working on code. But, when I am not working, I try to get in some game time with my boys. Being a PC gamer, I prefer a mouse and keyboard and do not like to play with a gamepad. I need them to work flawlessly. I am not sure what causes the random jump. I do not know if it is a driver issue (I have the latest driver from Corsair), Windows 10, or interference from my router or wireless headphones. I have already had to change some settings in my router to prevent my wireless headphones from constantly cutting in and out. So, I can't blame Corsair for this issue....yet. The Dark Core SE could be experiencing interference with my router or headphones. I have not tried the bluetooth connection yet and I might to to place my wireless router further away from my PC to see if I can resolve the issue. With many devices going wireless, however, the signal issues need to be sorted out between manufacturers.

While I will admit the Corsair Dark Core SE needs a little bit of work, I think it performs well overall. I do not notice any lag in games and the battery life is very good. It is comfortable to use, not as comfortable as Corsair's M65 Pro RGB, but it offers more freedom. I love Logitech's MX Master 2S mouse and how it is able to switch between two or three PCs at once using an adapter, bluetooth connections and Flow software. I have not experienced any interference issues with the Master 2S, however it is not a gaming mouse. Hopefully Corsair can come up with something similar in the future, as long as it is not another software that I have to install on my PC. They should try to integrate it into LINK or CUE. I will be testing battery life (exact time it lasts in hours), trying to remove any signal issues and then report it in a future blog post.
